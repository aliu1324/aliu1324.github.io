Chapter 1

I. Characteristics of the Curriculum

Language is the most important form and medium of communication in human society, an important component of civilization. The use of language with aural, oral, writing, reading and literary activities in daily lives, work place and academic environment exists in every field of society.

The Chinese language curriculum is a comprehensive and practical curriculum in learning the application of the mother tone. A combination of utility and humanities is a fundamental characteristic of the course. Students should utilize real-life scenarios to independently practice languages, cumulate language usage experiences, familiarize selves with the characteristics and patterns of the Chinese language, deepen understanding and appreciation of the language, develop languages skills, refine critical thinking skills, elevate personal virtues, raise the socialist values with Chinese characteristics, cultivate refined artistic appreciation and acquire broad cultural understanding along with recognition of multiculturalism.

The standardized Chinese curriculum should, on the basis of the mandatory education, develop Chinese proficiency, personal morale, literacy of sciences and humanities, foundation for comprehensive yet individualistic character of a learner for the sake of conserving and developing the Chinese culture, ethnic unity and national creativity.

II. Guiding Principles

1. Foster character through virtues, strengthen cultural self confidence, fully embrace the morale building purpose of the curriculum

The Chinese language is the spiritual homeland of the Chinese people and the curriculum is naturally positioned to preserving and promoting Chinese e culture, revolutionary culture, progressive socialism, while developing cultural self confidence and cultural creativity.

The curriculum, in accord with values of socialism with Chinese characteristics, must attempt to foster character through virtues, disseminate the Chinese spirit, educate with socialist core values, promote loyalty to to the Chinese civilisation, the motherland, the Chinese people, the Communist Party of China and a optimistic outlook on life and values, leading students to develop independent thinking and value systems, strengthen resolve for the historical responsibility of great revival of the Chinese people and a keen civic duty. Linking the curriculum to students' character development, the later should be lead to actively social participation and taught an understanding of nature, society, self and life planning, further embracing the comprehensive character building purpose of the curriculum.

2. Promote reform of the chinese language curriculum based on character building

Continue holistic education and promote cultural confidence

As the education sector and the society progress, the Chinese language curriculum should place a greater emphasis on the character building. It is necessary to further revise the purpose and content to pursue the overt knowledge skills but emphasis more on the inert values, also to pay attention to the changes of an information ear; through reforms, students should experience various guides and cultivations in the curriculum, progressive merging and internalisation of comprehensive virtues to read them for the value system, moral character and habits of the modern society.

With emphasis on principled education, further reforms of the Chinese language curriculum

The curriculum should further lead students to attain language skills, cultivate sense of language, master fundamentals of the language, foster healthy learning habits, improve ability to use the mother tongue. Language skills are closely linked to logics and the curriculum must teach reasoning skills; the curriculum is also a pathway to foster refined and individual tastes in art and culture where students should experience beauty in learning the language for creativity. Language is a mirror of social and cultural progress. Students should actively learn the noble Chinese traditional culture and revolutionary culture, absorbing the best of global cultures and contribute towards the building and promotion of socialism with Chinese characteristics.

Promote practice of Chinese, changing the way students learn.

As a curriculum that requires large amounts of practice, the curriculum should emphasize the importance of application. Since digital resources and real-life applications are abundant, the curriculum should increase the willingness of the students to learn and utilize Chinese through reading and analyzing, expressing and communicating, organizing and exploring, thus allowing them to become accustomed to the grammar and able to handle the language with greater confidence. This will train their abilities, developing ideas, habits, empathy, attitude and values along the way.

The curriculum should also remain flexible to mentor the people that modern society needs. The student should be guided to notice and recognize sensitive questions in utilizing Chinese, and to discover innovative ways to express themselves and solve problems.

Emphasize the flexibility of the curriculum based on time periods, making it open, diverse and disciplined.

The standardized high-school Chinese curriculum should adapt to the many needs of society, and what students wish to learn. Carefully choose the learning contents, revolutionize the methods of leaning to ensure that every student acquires the necessary language abilities. It should help students recognize their current strengths and how their skill sets should develop from there, stimulating their interest and potential, allowing them to expand and express their individuality in a multi-cultural, multi-media platform.

The standardized high-school Chinese curriculum should have a relatively fixed structure, but flexible mechanism in application.The teachers should find their own resources to carry out the curriculum in an innovative manner, increasing their own abilities while embracing the new digital age, actively using new technology and methods to create an open, diverse and disciplined curriculum structure and allowing the student's character development and enhancement to keep up with the needs of society.


Language is the most crucial media of communication and carrier of information for human society; and it is an irreplaceable part of our humanity. The use of language, including listening, speaking, reading, and writing, exists in every area of our society.

Chinese Literature is a curriculum which teach the comprehensiveness and practical value of the use of Chinese. The combination of practice and humanity is the fundamental quality of this curriculum. The Chinese Literature curriculum should guide students, under real life conditions, to cumulate language use experience, build love and interpretations towards Chinese, and develop the ability to use Chinese through practice; meanwhile, it should help candidates to enrich their ability of critical thinking, thinking quality as well as their appreciations for arts and the core value of Socialism; their understanding of cultural knowledge, and the diversity of the Chinese culture.




Chapter 2: Subject qualities and curriculum objectives

I. Subject Qualities

Subject qualities, or learning outcomes, refer to the values and abilities students cultivate through learning certain subjects. Learning outcomes in Chinese language and literature are cultivated through active engagement with course materials and can be applied in real-world contexts. The four major learning outcomes in the Chinese curriculum include “Language construction and application”, “Cognitive development”, “Appreciation for artistic and creative expression”, and “Understanding of Chinese culture”.

1. Language construction and application

“Language construction and application” refers to the ability for students to cultivate understanding for characteristics and patterns within the Chinese language and apply them accurately to real-life contexts. The skill of effective communication is fostered through experiential learning, active accumulation and organization of knowledge within the Chinese language.

2. Cognitive development

“Cognitive development” in the Chinese curriculum refers to the skillset that includes intuition, imagination, logical reasoning, persuasion, and creative expression. These qualities help foster critical thinking, creativity, vividness, and adaptability in the usage of the Chinese language.

3. Appreciation for artistic and creative expression

“Appreciation for artistic and creative expression” refers to students' accurate, positive interpretations of artistic beauty within Chinese literature. This is cultivated through experiencing art and through active literary analysis. In achieving this learning outcome, students also become more adept at creative self-expression and appreciating artistic beauty.

4. Understanding of Chinese culture

“Understanding of Chinese culture” refers to promoting traditional Chinese culture, revolutionary culture, contemporary socialist culture, cultivating understanding and empathy towards the cultures of different ethnic and geopolitical groups, broadening cultural horizons, enhancing cultural awareness, and advocating “Socialism with Chinese Characteristics”. In doing so, this learning outcome aims to foster greater appreciation towards the Chinese language with respect to its underlying cultural dimensions. Instructors are further reminded to reject cultural nihilistic ideals.

II. Curriculum Objectives

Through reading, literary critique, communication, organizing thoughts and investigating Chinese literary texts, students are able to achieve the four learning outcomes mentioned previously; Doing so helps establish cultural identity, promote the core tenets of socialism, cultivate positive personal prospects, and foster holistic and long-term personal development amongst students.

  1. Language construction. Accumulating sufficient knowledge and experience with the Chinese language, hence cultivating fluency. Drawing connections between concepts, fully grasping the patterns inherent to the Chinese language.
  2. Effective communication. Being able to use one's understanding of language patterns and context to fluently convey meaning through either spoken or written text. To be able to understand, analyze, and critique Chinese text types based on context and cultural/historical background.
  3. Organization of knowledge. To be able to organize and draw connections between different knowledge in the Chinese language to form a cohesive structure of understanding. Such structure enables one to readily access accumulated knowledge and apply them in real life situations.
  4. Enhanced imagination and creativity. To be able to intuitively experience Chinese language and literature. To sharpen one's intuition of the use of Chinese language in real-life and literary contexts through active reading, analysis, communication, organization, and investigation.
  5. Cultivating logical reasoning. To effectively recognize, summarize, analyze, and compare/contrast fundamental language and literary features. The ability to clearly articulate one's findings and express one's opinion regarding selected texts. Using patterns in the Chinese language and logic to assess the accuracy and descriptiveness of the use of words in certain texts. Being able to coherently convey personal understanding and critically analyze texts.
  6. Enhancing cognitive development. To critically reflect on personal experiences with the Chinese language, thereby adding depth, adaptability, creativity, and criticality in one's way of thinking.
  7. Promoting a profound appreciation for the Chinese language.
  8. Promoting appreciation for Chinese literary works. To recognize and contemplate the unique beauty inherent to Chinese literature. Using correct attitudes and values to appreciate and critique literary works from different historical contexts and styles.
  9. Improving creative self-expression. The ability to use the Chinese language to articulate one's feelings, attitudes, and values in an original fashion.
  10. Promote Chinese culture. Through learning the Chinese language, one becomes more aware and learns to appreciate the profound depth and breadth of Chinese cultural values. To encourage cultural self-confidence/identity, understanding, affirmation, and enthusiasm. To further advocate Chinese traditional and revolutionary culture.
  11. Embracing diversity of cultures. Through learning Chinese, students become more respectful and accepting of other ethnic/geopolitical/national groups and their respective cultures.
  12. Engagement with contemporary culture. To follow and actively engage in contemporary cultural dissemination and interactions. Through the use of the Chinese language, students become more aware of their cultural identities and thus become responsible members of society. Students recognize their role and civic duty to promote Chinese national rejuvenation.



Chapter 3: Curriculum Structure

Design Basis

  1. Using the ideals of Socialism with Chinese characteristics, establish the grounds for fostering holistic yet individualistic development, catering to each students' needs.
  2. Using the subject qualities and curriculum objectives, create “Learning Objective Groups” amongst students. Learning Objective Groups are responsible for organizing knowledge and learning materials, deciding on effective learning techniques, and guiding students to achieve the four learning outcomes. Students in Learning Objective Groups collaborate in their studies of a variety of texts, including those that fall in “contemporary media”, “traditional literature”, and “narrative” categories. Such collaboration aims to foster basic communication skills, inquisitive thinking, creativity, and cross-cultural interactions in the learning process.
  3. “Self-directed learning”, “Collaboration”, and “Inquiry” are the three primary modes of learning Learning Objective Groups hope to achieve. Rather than helping students learn specific aspects of the Chinese curriculum in isolation, Learning Objective Groups instead strive for holistic development through fostering multi-dimensional growth in areas such as creative expression, cultural understanding, and communication skills.
  4. Overall structure of the curriculum should reflect a degree of breadth of knowledge and opportunity for more depth. Different Learning Objective Groups are responsible for studying different material. As such, Learning Objective Groups are subdivided into those that study mandatory material and those that move on to study electives. Groups focusing on mandatory material should aim to achieve the four fundamental learning outcomes and consolidate a basic framework of knowledge in Chinese, hence establishing sufficient breadth of knowledge. Those focusing on electives are encouraged to dive deeper into different aspects of the subject depending on the students' interests and needs. Such learning process is thus both holistic and caters to the individual's needs.

Structure

The Chinese curriculum for public high schools consist of compulsory, optional-compulsory, and elective courses. Each course category should consist of 7-9 Learning Objective Groups. Additionally, material regarding traditional Chinese culture, contemporary revolutionary culture, and Chinese socialist culture should be included in each of the course categories.

The 7 compulsory courses are as follows: “Book reading and discussions”, “Contemporary cultural engagement”, “Multi-media studies”, “Language acquisition, organization and inquiry”, “Literary readings and writing”, “Critical reading”, and “Applicable reading and communication”.

The 9 optional-compulsory courses include: “Book reading and discussions”, “Contemporary cultural engagement”, “Multi-media studies”, “Language acquisition, organization and inquiry”, “Classics of traditional Chinese culture”, “Works of Chinese revolutionary culture”, “Contemporary Chinese works”, “Foreign literature”, “Scientific and cultural research”.

The 9 electives are: “Book reading and discussions”, “Contemporary cultural engagement”, “Multi-media studies”, “Inquiry on Chinese characters”, “Inquiry on traditional Chinese culture”, “Inquiry on Chinese revolutionary culture”, “Inquiry on contemporary Chinese works”, “Cross-cultural studies”, “Chinese Academic research”.

Academic credits and course selection

Regarding the distribution of academic credits, students are required to complete 8 credits worth of compulsory courses, 6 credits of optional-compulsory courses, and 12 credits of electives.

For clarification, compulsory courses are, by definition, required for every high school student to take. Additionally, optional-compulsory courses are taken according to the students' personal interests and/or based on graduation requirements whereas electives can be freely chosen. For optional-compulsory and elective courses, instructors are encouraged to advise students based on how each student wishes to direct his/her personal learning.

Chapter 4: Learning Objective Groups

Objective Group 1: Discussion and Analysis for entirety of a book

This objective group is aimed at leading the students through through analysis of literatures to broaden literary perspective, build literature experience, shape personal reading methodologies, improve literary appreciation, curate critical analysis and study of Chinese traditional, revolutionary and progressive socialist values and correct values.

This objective group consists of core, optional core and optional parts.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Through reading, understand the techniques to reading literature and build individual experiences. Emphasize existing reading experiences and with regard to various reading goals, comprehensively understand plots and quick reading techniques to understand the text and grasps its core themes and underlying essence.
    • Within the mandated list, choose a novel. Throughly read the book and understand its themes and artistic values. Via personally connected stories, characters, scenes, language, appreciate literatures, closely examine the art of of language, study emotionally charged scenes and its artistic structures. Comb through the characterisations to emphasise and appreciate them. Encourage students to discover the character's philosophies, understand the literature's messages and analyse its literary values.
    • Within the mandated list, choose an academic work. Throughly read an academic work. Guide the students to highlights of the book and formulate a systematic outline that summarise the book with its key views and values. Read appropriate context of the book and understand its academic perspective and values. Through repeated reading and analysis, explore the language and logic of the book.
    • Using the tables of contents, preface and foot notes, students should learn to use appendix, contextual informations and reviews to form a complex and sophisticated understanding of the work.
    • Students should relate to their personal experience and enjoy the process fo reading to learn and develop their mind with correct values. They should will, in their own language, write summary, notes and reviews to be shared orally, written or other media.
  2. Teaching Guidance：This objective group is part of the core curriculum and assigned *one* credit and *eighteen *teaching hours. It should include one full length novel and an academic work. The focus is on leading students to build reading skills and experiences to be applied in optional mandatory and optional units. No extra credit is given when the same experiences and techniques are applied in optional mandatory and optional units.
    • The chosen texts to read should posses exemplar language and rich content with complex thematic values and cultural values. With respect to the students' lives and developmental need, the works should reflect noble Chinese traditional, revolutionary and progressive socialist values. The 3-5 texts could be chosen from textbook's chosen extracts, or could be chosen collectively with students. Students will choose one to read and group with others with same text to discuss.
    • The classes can be spread over two semesters for concentrated study over each single text. In their repeated reading, a few key questions should be concentrated on each reading. Students should place difference levels of emphasis on different passages and take notes on individual though process and opinions.
    • The entirety of the book should be read individually outside of class along with notes and discussion,. Teachers should not limit nor replace such individual process with lectures. The teachers' tasks are to guide key study topics, organise activities and lead further discussion and contemplation. Teachers should use their own literary experiences to answer the questions and take part in discussions without patronizing the students.
    • Teachers should recognise student experience and success in reading and communicate these techniques to the class while protecting and supporting individual opinion and understanding of students.

Objective Group 2: Participation in contemporary culture

This objective group is aimed at leading students to acknowledge and participate in contemporary cultural activities, to learn analysis and critique of cultural phenomenons, to actively participate in promotion of progressive socialist values with Chinese characteristics and building cultural confidence.

This objective group is covered in mandatory, optional mandatory and optional units.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Students should focus on specific cultural phenomenons, independently organizes materials, ascertain the research gaol, create the questionnaire outline, conduct interviews, record responses and conclude report on how to promote socialist Chinese values and contemporary aesthetic pursuit of modern China.
    • Students should focus on contemporaneous cultural life, explore cultural aspects of communities, research and organise contextual material, analyse and discuss the cultural life, traditions, social values, evolution of practices in a community, further autonomous promotion of socialist values. Using various media, students should observe popular culture, formulating key questions and discussion to explain such phenomenons and building cultural understanding and critical thinking skills.
    • Students should form communities of language learning, (literature club, newsletter club, etc.) while probing the societal practices through reading and communication to hone language skills and to broaden perspectives; through inquiries, watching performances and cultural charities, diversify language learning.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group awards *0.5 *credits and is assigned *9 *teaching hours. Teachers are free to organise with respect to practical constraints. While it *awards no credit *in optional mandatory and mandatory sections, it can be combined with any other objective groups.
    • With a focus on participative, immersive and explorative activities, link student's academic and personal growth; though open independent learning, lead students to actively participate in contemporary culture; combine written learning with explorative inquiry and contrasting analysis, balance study and promotion of culture and elevate comprehensive language skills.
    • Prompt students to independently create clubs and initiate language learning communities, such as literature clubs, debate, poetry contest and drama performances.
    • Use personal resources, school resources to study culture in life; utilize public libraries, museums, galleries, opera, cinema, historical sites and other cultural heritages to master culture perception.

Objective Group 3: Multimedia communication

This objective group is aimed at leading students to learn the information and its presentation in multimedia, to observe and contemplate difference use of language in various media, to categorise the findings and patterns of different media, to improve cross-media communication, to elevate skills of understanding, analysis and commentary of media content, to critically asses information with correct values and to foster pursuit for truth.

This objective group is covered in mandatory, optional mandatory and optional units.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Students will learn common media and language tools, grasping skills to acquire, process, communicate and utilise information on various media.
    • Students need to understand the difference sources of informations and be able to judge their credibility and biases for independent thinking.
    • On online literature and online popular culture, with respect to correct moral values, ascertain the influence of internet on language and literature to deepen appreciation for language.
    • Develop collective learning approach to multimedia
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group awards 0.5 credits and is assigned 9 teaching hours. Teachers are free to organise with respect to practical constraints. While it awards no credit in optional mandatory and mandatory sections, it can be combined with any other objective groups.
    • Teachers can lead students to independently choose texts to study, printed, digital, or even physical exhibitions.
    • Teachers should focus on student's areas of interests and teach how various text forms influence learning, working and living in modern society.
    • Through analysis of text examples, categorise different media and text forms and their characteristics on storage, transmission and communication of information. Learn to utilise the different media accordingly.
    • Teachers should guide students to learn the impact of text forms on language and elevate their skills to manipulate different media with respect to authenticity and truthfulness.

Objective Group 4: Systematic categorisation of language

This objective group is aimed at cultivating learning habits and skills in student on acquisition and categorisation of language knowledge, observation and exploration of language usage, examination of issues in language usage, autonomous learning of language, careful examination of patterns in language use, independent accumulation of language sensitivity and appreciation for the beauty of the mother tongue.

This objective group is covered in mandatory, optional mandatory and optional units.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Accumulate language usage and skills of the Chinese language and characters, understand the role of Chinese character in the evolution of the language, review previously learned Chinese characters and appreciate the Chinese ethnic characteristics of Chinese language and characters.
    • Though reading language understanding its meanings within contexts, understand the difference and similitude between language and linguistic communication.
    • Though reading classical Chinese, study the its words and meanings within various context, understand its difference and similitude with modern Chinese. To correctly understand Chinese classical literary works, understand the evolution of the Chinese language while avoiding the pitfall of reading classical Chinese through modern Chinese.
    • Via the process of dissecting sentence structure and grammatically incorrect sentences, comprehend the structural characteristics and function words of Chinese to deepen understanding of grammar. While learning literary works, observe the application of words and manipulation of sentence structures and similar details to discern the dynamic creativity of language.
    • Via communication in both oral and written forms, compare and contrast the two language for perceive their distinct styles.
    • Review and contemplate on individual writing experiences, formulate writing logic and mastery of vocabularies, improve application of language and individualise communication style.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned *one* credit for *both *mandatory and optional mandatory units. No credit is offered for the optional units. It should be spread across the entire three years of high school and include contents both in-class and outside of class.
    • The learning process of language must be systematic, cohesive, planned, bit-by-bit and long-term. Students should enrich their vocabularies and communication skills while prepare for later reviews. Perseverance is essential in this process.
    • The distribution of teaching hours in the mandatory and optional mandatory units could be either concentrated or spread amongst other objective groups. It is up to the teacher to plan accordingly.
    • Within the mandatory and optional mandatory units, the content should be organically linked with other objective groups. Every objective group should contribute to the systematic categorisation of language by raising questions and providing practices, oral and written.
    • The systematic categorisation language is a process that should take place parallel to all learning. In the mandatory units, the focus is on notes. In the optional mandatory units, the focus is on writing practices.
    • The underlying characteristic of this unit is the repeated nature of learning for the sake of developing good senses of language and understanding of patterns, not entirety of knowledge. Do not interfere with individual learning through dogmatic teaching.
    • Reading the most appropriate texts for specific topics is essential to promoting in depth analysis of students.

Objective Group 5: Appreciation and creation of literature

This objective group is aimed at leading students to read prose, verse, novel, play and various literary works of various text medium, modern and classic, Chinese and foreign, to elevate artistic appreciation of languages, emotions and literary features for the sake of creating literature, critiquing literature, appreciating art and communicating understandings. Half of the literary texts read in this unit should compose of classical Chinese texts.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Conduct in-depth reading of literary works to appreciate the artistic images, to acknowledge the use of language, to ascertain the themes of the text and to understand the motives of creation. Relate with life experiences and previously read or written works.
    • With respect to different literary techniques of prose, verse, novel and play, approach the text from perspectives of language, imagery, emotion, themes and conception of work to obtain aesthetic literacy.
    • With respect to previously read work, categorise the creation of prose, verse, novel and play. Write literature with individual medium of choice and share the experience with peers. Attempt to continue writing literature.
    • Conceive habits of writing synopsis and notes in the reading process or other texts such as reviews, critique and commentary whenever possible for sharing personal growth and understanding with peers.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned 2.5 credit, 45 teaching teaching hours with no less than 8 writing assignments (excluding notes and synopsizes)
    • Texts can be sorted for compare and contrast to instigate curiosity for reading and learning.
    • Students should be responsible for reading, discussion and writing and assume autonomy in the learning process.
    • Teachers should support students by providing guiding questions and reading tips, facilitating group activity for communication, participating as an equal partner, provide a wide range of texts in a planned manner, including texts that reflect upon the Chinese Revolution, texts that concern the contemporary literary scene. Teachers should encourage students to individually conduct poetry contest, literature festival, drama performance and other activities to diversify students' aesthetic literacy, promote creative works and invite self-criticism.

Objective Group 6: Speculations and communication

This objective group is aimed at promoting critical reading and communication for the sake of positivist, logical, critical and explorative skills. Skills to discern the nature of things and their properties are essential to critical thinking skills. Classical Chinese texts should take up half of of the total texts read in class.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Read exemplary argumentative essays, of Chinese and foreign and of classical and contemporary natures. Discern characteristics of argument, tone and language of the author. Read editorial articles on recent issues and learn the methods and perspectives of commenting news. While reading various texts, develop scepticism, critical thinking and objective readings skills of different perspectives.
    • Learn to communicate, clarify and demonstrate individual perspectives with correct arguments, language and logic. Learn to approach questions from different dimensions. Learn to rebut and logically convince others.
    • Discuss and debate around topics of interest. Learn to debate with reason in a orderly manner with respect and focus.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned *1.5 *credit, 27 teaching hours with no less than 3 writing assignments and 3 topical discussion or debate activities.
    • Learnings should be centred on topics or themes that are essential and interested to students in their live, study, modern society or past. Students should categorise argumentative methods by reading selected argumentative essays and apply them orally and written.
    • Lessons must be guide students to develop dialectical and critical thinking skills in both process and methods. With respect to issues encountered in reading and communication, teach students appropriate dialectic philosophical knowledge which must be simple and applicable to utilise in real life.

Objective Group 7: Application of reading and communication

This objective group is aimed at leading students to study common contemporary application of writing, including independent reading, and to understand of these text for real life application. Through this objective group, students should diversify their literacy and experiences and elevate their communication and reading skills.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Learn to observe modern society through different lenses and master common text forms of non literary texts.
    • Learn to use simple language to elaborate complex concepts.
    • The texts to be studied can be social contracts such as meeting summary, negotiation dialogues, contracts, rules, project plan, speeches and common text. They can also be of various news media, such as news, communication, interview, investigation, commentary, hosts, televised speech and internet texts. They may eve be educational texts.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned *1* credit, 18 teaching hours
    • Lessons should focus on investigative activities lead by students with appropriate amount of reading, investigation, discussion, writing and oral discussion.
    • Social contract texts should be studied in social investigation.
    • New media texts should be learned in analysis of contemporary media. For example, students can choos to analyse a week of content from a newspaper or a website and analysis its format, language, values and perspectives for written reports. Attempt to write both traditional and new media texts.
    • Select a popular non fiction book on social or natural science for individual study.

Objective Group 8: Classical Chinese Culture

This objective group is aimed at leading students to develop cultural literacy, ethnic aesthetic taste, appreciation for Chinese traditional culture through readings of classical traditional Chinese literature.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Select exemplary noteworthy works of different types from various era of Chinese culture to discern their values, artistic taste and cultural values.
    • Study traditional cultural works through specific social-cultural lense to objectively evaluate the roles the works played in development of Chinese culture.
    • Categorise common classical Chinese terms, function words and linguistic quirks to distinguish between modern and classical Chinese.
    • Select one work to commentate from one or various perspectives.
    • Study traditional literary texts to improve writing skills.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned *2* credits and *36* teaching hours.
    • A keen sense for language is an essential target of this objective group, one that grow out of reading experiences of traditional Chinese texts.
    • Teach the use of various tools for reading texts, including footnotes and tool books and connection with previously read classical Chinese texts for categorisation of classical Chinese language.
    • From various perspectives and dimensions, lead students to optimise reading strategy to establish a minor understanding of traditional Chinese culture.
    • On the basis of wide reading, facilitate discussions on cultural values of these works from different time perspectives.
    • Encourage students to utilise academic sources when reading both in libraries and online while also consistently taking notes.

Objective Group 9: Chinese Revolutionary Texts

This objective group is aimed at reading and discussing revolutionary texts that are models of language use, complex in logic and representative of their zeitgeist for the sake of understanding the revolutionary heroism, sacrifices in patriotism. Students should formulate correct values while developing literacy across all areas.

This unit consists of mandatory, optional mandatory and optional units.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Read the prose and verses written by the Revolutionary forerunners to appreciate their noble revolutionary emotions. Read works that reflect these revolutionary principles, specifically focussing on the party leaders' leadership of the people in revolution and reformation. Connect with individual experiences to further personal understanding of the works.
    • Read essays on the revolutionary sprits, with an emphasis on those with highly regarded theoretical frameworks. Appreciate the combination of articulate lucid logics and worthy virtuous spirits of revolution.
    • Read news, communique, reports, speeches, interviews and other texts of the revolutionary tradition and learn their writing techniques.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned *0.5 *credit and 9 teaching hours.
    • When choosing reading materials, neglect neither the language values nor the thematic role. At the same time, seek to diversify with new texts with real applicability.
    • Teachers should use various methods to facilitate sharp critical thinking in discussions of relevant interesting topics. Allow students to formulate individual different opinions but invite experts with correct opinions to assist students in arriving at the correct conclusion.
    • Emphasise the role of contexts in understanding the texts through trips, interviews and investigation of the heroism within the context of revolution. Potentially, work cross-disciplinary with history or geography.

Objective Group 10: Modern and Contemporary Chinese Literature

This objective group is aimed at studying modern and contemporary Chinese literature including those of socialist values after the reformation. It is aimed at raising literacy of modern literature.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Read selected works and grasp their inner messages and artisitc values. Read works of at least 10 renowned author, poet and playwright.
    • Observe the contemporary literature scene and selectively read recent influential works and their reception.
    • Nurture a habit of writing reading notes that should summarise and reflect individual thoughts. Commentate on works of choice.
    • Practice creative writing on medium of choice.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This unit is assigned 0.5 credit and 9 teaching hours.
    • The Reading material could be standalone short works or selected from collections of works or excerpts from long works. Works selected should complement each other thematically, of which there must be works representing socialist spirits.
    • Guarantee class time for individual reading and design activities to further character development. These activities should be diverse and interesting, e.g. poetry reading events and drama acts.
    • Guarantee class time for discussion and facilitate exchange of ideas and understanding. Emphasise reflection after reading and encourage such reflections in activities.

Objective Group 11: Foreign Literature

This objective group is aimed at studying foreign literature to understand different countries and ethnic groups at their different social era, developing appreciation for diversity of culture and tolerance for reading foreign literature.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Read classical foreign literature and understand their importance and value.
    • Nurture a habit of writing reading notes that should summarise and reflect individual thoughts. Commentate on works of choice.
    • Discuss cultural similarity and differences within literature, respect diversity and elevate cultural literacy.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned 1 credit and 18 class hours.
    • Invite in-depth reading to grasp the emotional undertone and thematic messages. Design challenging study tasks to encourage interests in studying works of different eras and countries.
    • Relating to student's knowledge of world history and geography, deepen global understanding.
    • Organise students by interests of topics or author to facilitate discussions.

Objective Group 12: Scientific Works

This objective group is aimed at studying works of social science and natural science to teach communication in the field of science and promote scientific exploratory attitudes.

  1. Study Goal and Content
    • Choose texts from all spectrums of social and natural science, whole journals or excerpts. The texts should avoid difficult technical languages and details.
    • Write outlines and reading notes. Study the process of summarisation, categorisation, reasoning, proof and other scientific methods. Appreciate the cogent arguments, methodical logic and accurate concise language.
  2. Teaching Guidance: This objective group is assigned *2* credits and *36* teaching hours.

Chapter 5: Academic quality

The meaning of “academic quality”:

Academic quality shows in the performance of a student after graduating from this course. The standards of academic quality will be decided through the core accomplishments explained in the previous chapters, combined with the content of this course, they will give a good idea of what level the student has reached within this course. Different degrees of success in this course will have its own characteristics. Using some standards, these different levels of academic success can be decided.






The relationship between academic quality and test scores:

The standard divides student results into five levels. Levels one and two are the standards for compulsory courses, Levels two and three are the standards for higher level courses, and level five is the standard for optional courses. Level two acts as a basis for high-school level Chinese tests, level four acts as a basis for the acceptance of students for higher level schools and level five acts as a basis for students with interest in Chinese courses, and the student’s success in the course should be considered by higher level schools.




Chapter 6: Further suggestions

On teaching

1.Elaborate the unique purpose of the Chinese curriculum, and promote the development of students' ability of core Chinese usage ability.

The Chinese curriculum for normal high schools should focus on guiding the development of students' emotions, stands, and values. Teachers should pay attention on the perspective of the educational contents to imbue and nurture the students with the Chinese curriculum. The instructor should respect the unique learning experience of the student and guide his/her students to be nurtured by the Chinese curriculum, gaining art appreciating experience, establishing a welly-established self-cultivation, and developing a morally acceptable set of values for the world, the life, and the individuality.

2.Fully understand the uniquenesses of the assignment groups, and neatly manage the relation between different *assignment* groups.

There are 18 assignment groups included in the normal Chinese curriculum. While each assignment group has its own purpose and content, all the assignment groups are entwined and logically connected. Teachers could be organized, stylistic, and creative in the lessons based on the characteristics of the assignment group, progress made by the students, and their own academic specialities. The educator should fully aware of the uniqueness of each assignment group as well as their functions and dispositions and neatly manage the relation between assignment groups to avoid any omission; bewared of the distinctions on the principle, presentation, and range of each assignment group and to avoid simple repetitions.

3.Create a comprehensive learning environment: promote individual, collaborative, and research learning skills.

Educators should pay attention on the changing in his/her students learning methods, designed learning activities, guide and organize, and examine students' learning outcomes. Depends on students needs, educators should establish a learning environment which can guide the students to participate throughly and meaningfully based on different assignment groups. Educators should help students breaking the barrier between Chinese and other subjects, and bring the Chinese curriculum into daily lives through these learning activities which comprise of cultures, ancient and modern, Chinese and foreign, and usage of speaking, listening, reading, and writing skills. Reorganize and consolidate the curriculum: help students to turn knowledge into their skills, to apply their methods, emotions, attitudes and values into the learning process, by doing topic reading, comparison reading, topic learning and projective learning etc. therefore enhance students' ability of using Chinese.

Encourage students to, based on personal interests, capacities, and specialities, purposefully choosing their studying contents, developing self-monitory skill and learning management, to explore personal learning methods. Strictly follow the principles of the Chinese curriculum, and neatly manage the amount of output content without increasing the academic pressure blindly. Meanwhile, encourage students, in the realm of “possible” and interest, to reach for a higher standard.

Protect the curiosity, passion for learning, encourage personal reading, free expression, guiding them to experience of process of finding-solving problems, based on students' personal developments and characteristics. Promoting topic studies that are based on the assignment groups; organizing group projects, and communications based on language and culture, classic literary works, scientific essays, to encourage cooperations between students(any kind) and displaying and communicating studying results. Enhance teaching quality by using the appropriate technology. Teachers have to focus on leading students, on the foundation of self--learning , to learn, share, communicate, and collaborate, mastering different learning methods, and excelling on creativity and practical skills.

4.Manage core curriculum and optional units as an entirety; enhance the connection and similarities between courses.

The educator has to connect and reorganize the content of compulsory courses, optionally compulsory courses, and elective courses. Manage the relation between elective courses and compulsory courses while focus on the connection between the unique assignment groups for different type of courses.

The compulsory course must be based on mutual foundations, focus on the accumulating daily linguistic knowledge, and build a solid foundation for elective courses. The education must focus on developing the basic usage of linguistic knowledge. thinking and deducing, reading and understanding of literary works, as well as culture, and creativity.

Elective courses must emphasize the distinction and gradation of the course, encouraging individual explorations, and promoting students' potentials and interests. The class must bring students' ability of constructing and organizing language as well as critical thinking into a higher level. The optionally compulsory course has to focus on the “width” of the content while the elective course has to focus on the “depth” of the content.

5.Explore the shift of teaching method under this digitalized society.

Educators must not be content on Chinese educations, and worship no technology; educators must neatly manage the relation between digital technologies and Chinese curriculum, and use technologies appropriately. Educators have to create the appropriate content for Chinese curriculum, forming meaningful, interactive learning environments to help students devote themselves into practical Chinese; use technologies to polish in-class activities, guiding students to experience a diverse learning process, encouraging students to learn Chinese initiatively in the natural environment to realize the encoding an use of knowledge. Educators must explore progressively on the digitalization revolution of Chinese education based on the development of internet, contributing to the lessons, online and offline, with a learning environment that includes the internet. Further deduct the changes that must be made on teaching procedures, resource usage, educational support, and grading, exploring the appropriate teaching method in this digitalized world.

6.Develop the ability of initiating and designing courses while make improvements on courses.

Teachers should be aware of professional developments, trying to build educational communities; adopt and participate in the evolution of Chinese curriculum, learning, renewing, practicing, and improving the quality of education; be prepared to work with colleagues and students, and learn how to self reflecting through cooperative lesson preparations and case studies, to improve through teaching; follow the educational patterns in objective groups, consolidate lesson related resources, broaden the horizon of the students, and exceed daily efficiency; focus on the resources of the school/location and resources that revealed during the program, guide the students to discover real-life problems, working out activity topics, and enhance on the awareness of Chinese usage in various circumstances, improving Chinese ability in multiple aspects.

On grading

1. Focus on the development of core values

The fundamental intention behind grading is to fully develop student's core values. Process of commenting should accompany the student's learning progress and be based around activities such as reading and understanding, expressing and communicating, and organizing and researching. Thus, fully investigate student's core value development progress in specific language learning environments and activities.

The grading must functions as a mean to check, access, reflect, encourage, discriminate, and select, rather than just a process of selecting and discriminating. The grading process must not only focus on the learning result, but also the nature of the mean. Focus on guiding and encouraging self-learning through grading.

Chinese teachers should purposefully use the process and result of grading to discover the characteristics and problems of the student meanwhile inciting the motivation for studying by proposing highly personalized suggestions.

2. Effectively utilize grouped study goals characteristics

Grading should address characteristics of of target category. The focus and content of every category is distinctive while interconnected. Comments should project each focus while considering that connection in order to coherently provide grading in agreement with the target of each target category.

Commentators should be aware of characteristics of the Chinese language practices and take notes on students' participation, unique mind set, communication, problem solving, and creative thinking capabilities to fairly record students' complete learning progress within each learning target category.

3. Encourage diversification when commenting

The Chinese grading should be direct to students while acknowledging their dominance in their Chinese career. Comments should emphasize on the self development process. On the basis of meeting elementary targets, comments should be personalized in area such as interests and performance to support different intended growth. When grading specific learning assignments, the Chinese instructor should provide detailed comments alongside with practicable directions to improve. Thus, guiding students through comments, adjustments to learning process, organizing learning methods, and setting learning targets.

4. Use the appropriate grading method

The core literacy should be assessed under the condition of Chinese assignment.
